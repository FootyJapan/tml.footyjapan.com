MARITIZIO START THEIR CLIMB.

March 6th 2005 Nodai, Maritizio 3 v 2 BFC
Nodai was the setting for a very importing game which Maritizio's simply had to win to sustain some form of hope for staying in contention to escape the relegation zone. On a fine Sunday morning therefore, the scene was set for this showdown with BFC who could be forgiven for assuming that it would be just another walk in the park. Indeed, how wrong this turned out to be. From the kickoff a familiar pattern from Maritizios point of view had already developed, whereby it was all hands on deck to stem the initial early controlled onslaught from BFC. Mid way through the half however, Maritizio's fortuitousness came to bare once again, when a penalty was awarded for a seemingly unnecessary challenge by the BFC keeper 'Sid'. Vernon Gill stood fast to punish BFC with a precise & cleverly placed shot with the BFC keeper not hardly moving. Confidence then reined through the now very much up-beat Maritizio side that then went on to create several more chances before half time. Both teams fairly even at this stage.
It was the second half however that produces a more thrilling story to tell. Early during the second half BFC seized on a needless error by the Maritizio goalkeeper which provided a gifted header for the BFC forward. All square it is again then. 10 minutes or so were to follow before Maritizio showed reminisance of their former sprit which they had yet all too often failed to manifest. A long ball was played from midfield to the Maritizio left wing who was able to outrun the slower BFC defense and calmly slide the ball past an on-coming BFC keeper. With only 10miuntes or so left to play BFC heads noticeably slumped, clearly attributed to their own frustration with not being to penetrate the now sturdy & assured Maritizio defense, with sloppy passing. Now, seemingly in real despair BFC adopted a 'Charge of the Light Brigade' style attack, throwing everything at Maritizio. This somehow ultimately paid off when too Maritizio's Shock/Horror BFC were awarded a penalty from an apparent push in back challenged from a throw-in. With only 2 minutes or so approximately left to play this was converted with ease to the elation of the BFC team. From the kickoff Maritizio's continued endeavor won them a corner with literally 1 minute on the clock left. BFC failed to deal with the corner , which resulted in a short sharp goalmouth scramble , the ball of which finding itself at the feet of a Mariizio defender which was slotted home, 2 yards from the goal line through the hapless Keeper's legs. Maritizio went ecstatic to say the least and 1 minute later the game was over. BFC walked away very frustrated in what could only be described as a lukewarm performance while Maritizio achieved exactly what the needed to, and can take great heart form the improved all round performance of the team. A betting man would tell you there's yet more to come from this Maritizio team.

Match Reporter: Vernon Gill
