Battling Sala Outclassed by BFC

YC&AC, Sunday 8th February

Well, the headline says it all.  Sala fought hard and battled admirably, but in the end, were outplayed by a classy intelligent footballing team and last year's deserved champions.  A typical laser beam shot from Carlos Aranda, a header from BFC new guy, Mike, and two poachers goals from Jon Day, proved more than enough for BFC to take the points. 
 
In fairness to Sala though, 4-0 was a harsh scoreline considering the 3rd and 4th goals were gifts coming from poor Sala mistakes.  And, continuing on a positive note, Sala's performance was a fitting answer to their Captain's request for more passion and fight than the team had shown the previous week against Kanto Celts.   
This match was definetely a step in the right direction for Sala, in terms of passion, spirit and hard running. 
 
The match began with Dave and Scotty both rolling up their sleeves and getting stuck right into BFC early on.  This encouraged the rest of the team, subs included, to do likewise.  Fine hard-working performances from Yusuke, Dan and Lenny helped Dave command the defence with typical ferocity and VOLUME (a new TML award, League's Loudest Swearer, should be created just to see who would come runner up to Dave). 
 
Despite Sala's spirited display they couldn't compete with BFC in terms ball  possession and attacking creativity.  In the first 30 mins John Day had a goal  (correctly) disallowed for offside, Taka saw a rocket shot crash off the bar, and, amazingly, one of BFC's own players cleared a goal-bound Aranda freekick off the Sala goal line.  Add to that several low drilled crosses which angled across Sala's 6 yrd box, albeit with no takers, and it was clear to see who held the attacking edge. 
 
At the other end Sala showed a lack of cohesion when attacking and they never really troubled the BFC keeper.  In the first half, Toby and Sam looked isolated up front, Shige was often forced deep to collect the ball, Take looked dangerous, as usual, on the right, but his final ball never had the precision needed to unlock a solid BFC defence, Brookey played well but always seemed to be smothered by numerous players before creating anything, and Pete, Scotty and Guido weren't dynamic enough to combine a solid defensive display with an attacking threat.  Finally, a mention for new boy, Rob, who will carry on the proud name of Harlow at Sala.  Expected to be an attacking threat in the future, he was too often called on to carry out defensive duties, like all of the Sala team. 
 
With 30 mins gone, the breakthrough came after another period of sustained BFC pressure.  Most of the Sala midfielders had been pushed back into the box helping out the defence, which left Aranda free and unmarked 20 yards from goal.  Given space and time, he drilled an unstoppable shot into the bottom corner.  Importantly, Sala's heads didn't go down but 5 minutes later they were beaten again from an inswinging freekick.  A BFC midfielder rose the highest and looped a header over Shun and into the net.  2-0 at halftime. 
 
In the second half Pete was given a more offensive midfield roll, which resulted in a much better attacking display.  Pete, Guido, Toby, Lenny and Stu all had shots which improved on Koji's lone effort in the first half.  BFC looked happy to sit on their lead, and possibly to just get off the pitch with all their limbs and reproductive apparatus intact as Sala continued to resemble a pack of pitbulls chasing a little bunny.

However, even though the second half was a more even contest, sadly for Sala, against a team of BFC's calibre you can't afford to make sloppy mistakes, and it was two of these that gifted BFC their 3rd and 4th goals, effectively ending the match.  First, Dan didn't clear a through ball, allowing John Day to ghost in and slot into an empty net.  Then, Shun fumbled a freekick and with the Sala defence looking around for a little bunny to massacre they failed to spot John Day, who reacted the quickest, and bundled the ball home from a yard.  4-0 BFC. 

So, another defeat for Sala this season and it's clear that there's still work to be done by all the players, especially with regard to attacking as a unit.  But they did improve in terms of passion, spirit, resolve, effort and defensive solidity.  Perhaps Sala's current situation was summed up best by BFC's captain when he said after the game "It won't be long before Sala's are back at the top."  
 
We all certianly hope so.

Report by Scotty Twain Hotty
