Sala n BFC Share Spoils - Penalty Furore!!


Fukuda Denshi, Saturday, June 6th
What would football be without controversy??  Probably NOT the best sport in the world ever.  However, what would football be with some controversy but no diving??  Definitely, the best sport in the world ever....... 
 
After a ding donger battle royal both teams went away from this match with a point,  having served up an enthralling 1-1 draw.  But, it must be said, Sala went away with something extra.  It was a bad taste in their mouths.  What was that taste you ask??  That of another man's urine in their Carlsberg!!  Read on readers for there is a twist in this sordid tale.

Early possession and 3 or 4 corners saw BFC in the driving seat for the first 5 mins, but after this, Sala's tireless running up front and in midfield, started to cause the BFC defenders to make mistakes at the back, and the BFC midfield soon vanished like a ninja up a wizard's sleeve.  
 
From this point to half time Sala were in the ascendancy.  Rob Harlow, finishing the season on a hot streak, sent a blistering left foot shot whistling just over the BFC goal.  This was an ominous warning and sure enough he broke the deadlock minutes later.  Finding space on the left, Guido switched play with a brilliant crossfield ball to Take.  Take played it early to the ever industrious Pete Swinney who chipped it infield to Rob on the edge of the box.  Swinney gave Rob the shout to turn and before you could say "Watch out! There's a sniper active around here" Rob chested the ball on the turn and lashed a dipping right foot volley over the helpless BFC keeper. 
Sala 1-0.
 
Sala then came close from a corner with danger man, Dave, setting up Clarkey whose attempt was cleared from the 6 yard box.  Next, Scotty almost latched onto a tantalising ball across the 6 yard box from Take who was causing all sorts of trouble down the right flank.  Despite being on top though in the first half Sala lacked the quality required in the final third to extend their lead.
 
And so it was that Sala went in with their heads held high and a deserved 1-0 lead come the half time whistle.  

But, just like a threesome, football is a game of two halves, and in the second 45 mins BFC came out all guns blazing like a supercharged TNT guns of the Navarone powderkeg of football.  Whereas Sala were more like a new born armidillo.  All shell with only a few spikes worth mentioning.
 
Immediately, a BFC striker was sent clean through but he blazed over when a placed finish would have been far wiser.  Soon after, BFC's Shousuke dribbled in from the right and lashed a shot at the Sala goal.  Veteran keeper Andy Cross managed to get a scaley claw to it though, and pushed it onto the post.  
 
At this point Sala launched a series of counters.  Stu caused a BFC defender to miss clearing a long ball and Rob raced through on the keeper.  Looking odds on to score he was just about to pull the trigger when the BFC captain, James, made a great recovery tackle forcing the Sala striker to fluff his shot.  From the resultant corner, Stu proved that white men from Barrhead can jump, glancing a lovely header a bawhair above the top corner.  Then Rob fizzed another howitzer shot just off target.  
 
Normal service resumed though and BFC attacked again.  Another shot flashed in from the left this time, only for Andy "You ain't seen nothin yet" Cross to palm it onto his favourite left post.  Carlos Aranda was next to threaten the Sala goal with a peach of a curling shot from outside the box which thunked off the stanchion.  
 
At this point the seige on Sala's goal was getting ridiculous, with BFC having hit the post 3 times, possessed about 70% of the second half ball and caused about 8 corners,  irrespective of Sala's dangerous counters.  However, despite deserving a goal the score still remained at 1-0 to Sala.  And, it was for this reason that what happened next was so unfair on Sala, as no matter how much a team deserves an equaliser nobody ever deserves to score from a penalty gained through diving.  Unfortunatley, for Sala this is what happened.  One of BFC's strikers, who shall remain nameless, wriggled past 2 defenders in the box then took a theatrical tumble.  The referee made an honest call and called it the way he saw it; penalty.  But, to the group of Sala palyers camped in their own box it was a clear dive, and the BFC players close to the incident knew that, too.  Carlos stepped up and nonchalantly stroked home the spot kick.   
1-1.
 
Both teams looked for the winner in the last 10 mins but Sala's lack of quality in the final was again their main problem, and the game finished one apiece.
 
Anyway, it's started to rain and my soapbox is getting slippery, so I'm going to step down and end any foolish ranting before it really gets started.  So, sleep well readers, and don't worry as we'll all be back next year for another dose of the TML clap.  Well, everyone that is apart from the divers, I hope.  They're scabby enough already!!

Man of the Match - Sala's Back 3.
Sinner of the Match - Mmmmmm.
