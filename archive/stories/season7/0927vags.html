Vags Hold on for 2nd Victory

Hachioji Park, Sunday 27th September
The vagabonds made it two wins on the trot/bounce/spin, whatever you prefer, overcoming the Barbarians by 3 goals to 2 at Hachioji koen. Taking to the field in a kit that smelled of cats urine the vagabonds dominated the first half in terms of posession and territory and at times played some splendid link-up football. Garry Robertson - the poor man's Steve Glenn- was imperious at the back mopping up any danger that came our way. Going forward, Graham Burt was always available at right back and Nao, Ian, Phil, Muzzy and especially Takuma were putting the Barbarians defence under extreme pressure.
 
The breakthrough came after about 30 mins when Lyndsey - the vags player most able to secure a patent for his run with Monty Python's ministry of silly walks - delivered a pinpoint corner for Nao to nod home from point blank range. All was going pretty well and so some of the Barbarians were looking bemused as the vags gave a fairly negative half time team talk in full earshot.
 
The first 20 minutes of the 2nd half went like a dream for the vags. The game was pretty much decided after Richard Straughan's 'inspired substitutions' bringing on Matt and Amos. In fairness though the phrase  'inspired substitutions' is just some bollocks made up by commentator twats like Clive Tydlesley and the fact is that both Amos and Matt had to come on then or they wouldn't have had a game. Anyway they did well, Matt, who seems to be scoring for fun at the moment (another stupid cliche that, what else would he score for? indifference? world peace?), made it 2-0 with a header from close range before Muzzy made it 3 with a fine finish after a smart move that involved 4 vags players.
Game over? Unfortunately not, there's more chance of finding a clean sheet in a love hotel than there is with the vags, and so a 3-0 walk in the park turned into 3-2 with the battling Barbarians scoring a good first goal and then taking advantage of some sloppy defensive play to pile the pressure back on the vags. The grandstand finish never materialised, the two goals proved to be just a consolation for the Barbarians (who didn't actually look that consoled, in fact have any of you ever felt consoled by a 'consolation goal'?) though, as the vags stepped up a gear and saw the last 10 minutes or so out with comfort and should really have added to their tally.
A very good performance from the vags, who will look forward to welcoming back arguably their 3 best players in Steve Glenn, Paul Wadsworth and Japan's 7th most eligible bachelor as well as our prolific match reporter garry quinn, which all bodes well for the coming matches and match reports of the tml season. UP THE VAGS.

Report by Wayne Baxter.
