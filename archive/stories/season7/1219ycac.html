YC&AC Deadlocks Hibs 1-1 in 2009 Home Finale



YC&AC Sunrday 19th December.  
YC&AC closed their 2009 schedule with a 1-1 deadlock against the Hibs and remained unbeaten on the year for TML competition.
 
Despite a one-man disadvantage due to an early sending off, YC&AC held on quite tightly and managed well to keep the Hibs away from goal. And although with the initiative of the game mostly in Hib's hands, YC&AC was successful to nick a goal from Hitoshi Ono. A quick freekick taken by Anthony Savage sought the head of Kazuki Oshima before Ono's challenge.
 
However, 15 minutes to go in the 2nd half proved to be too long for the 10 men YC&AC squad to walk away with three points; minutes after, Tomomitsu Kunii, who created trouble for the YC&AC defense down the flank with Yohei Ota on the other side, neatly finished on a clean counterattack, 1-1.
 
Hibs tried for another, though YC&AC seemed to be happy with a point in hand; nothing special or noteworthy thereafter.
 
Finally, sayonara and farewell to Mike McGirr who is due to be leaving Tokyo soon. Encountering a perhaps tough yet fair tackle for a fifty-fifty ball at the beginning of the game somewhat bothered and occupied the 27 year old Irishman's (Englishman I believe – ed) mind throughout the entire game.  A few moans and groans towards the referee and a few choice challenges too far from the referee’s eagle eye followed the initial exchanges.  However, it seems that football is so fascinating it can surpass almost anyone's normal behaviour off the pitch. So we love you Mike, you are a great player. 

Report by Joe Takeda
