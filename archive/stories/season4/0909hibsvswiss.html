Swiss Hold Back Hungry Hibernian

St Mary's, Sat 9th Sept. Swiss vs. Hibernian is always a tough match, no different this time as the small pitch at St. Mary’s really makes it tight with some big lads on display. 
The game ended 0-0 but the Hibs. have to be disappointed not to have come away with the victory.

 Numerous chances in the box missed the target entirely (Bevan, Yama, Yohei, Mick) and some were just shot straight at Peter in goal.  One of the clear mishaps was Yohei who just kicked the ground instead of the ball in spite of being in oceans of space – the ball ended up rolling softly to Peter. 

The Swiss should be happy with the draw as a good first half really deteriorated in the 2H as a lack of fitness and perhaps a lack of ability in attack really forced the team to defend.  We threatened 2-3 times at best in a long 2H while the midfield was having difficulty keeping up with the waves of attacks coming back the other way.  Bevan, Tomo, Yohei, and Kuni kept flowing through and it was difficult as the match wore on.  Dave P. tried to drive through on counter attacks but they were lonely runs much of the time.  The defense did well to keep the ball out but Peter was called on too frequently as the numbers of Hibs. in space were just too much. 

Cho was great, marking Yohei throughout the game and Rafaelle was rabid as usual – Suzuki did well again at left back and he might find it difficult to get back into midfield with performances like that.  Some positives to take away were that ball moved around the pitch well when we could keep it and the team stayed positive throughout the game.  The Swiss need to look at the positives as we head back to the larger pitch at Oifuto for the next matchup. 

It certainly was a better start to the season than last year’s 1-5 demolition (by the Hibs. at St. Mary’s) so hopefully we can start working on that goal differential that already looms large.  Lastly, great match by Ricky the ref. who kept everyone honest all game.
Report – Capn. Kirk
