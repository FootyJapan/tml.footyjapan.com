Hibs' Nightmare at Hachioji Continues!

Hachioji Park, Sat 20th Jan. Hibs ran into a good wake up call for what they are going to face in their challenge in rest of TML4 season. Though all of depleted squad of bare 11 who showed up played quite well, even created some chances and came close to scoring a goal, BFC were the ones who played with more motivation and determinations throughout.
It was a one of those nights..... Just losing 6 key regular players meant no subs for Hibs. A Large Hachioji park and facing a much improved BFC, turned out to be a recipe for trouble.
5 munites into the match BFC marksman Jon Day executed a great first timer from a 45 degree angle. From then on, both sides worked hard to claim control in the middle of the park. Into the second half BFC Finnish keeper Jurki went out to a non-contact injury putting Al Himmer between the posts, which may have turned unfortunate for Hibs as striker Tomo Kinii's shots were athletically stopped on a few occasions by th 6 foot 6 giant. 
With 20 munites to go, Hibs went on to push Defender Jay Alabaster forward, but putting 7 men in attack to recover the deficit tured costly. BFC Shosuke Yamagishi and James Morson capitalized on 2 tired Hibs defenders out for a footrace and scored appropriately.
Credit to Hibs men for never giving up, specially excellent efforts from Mick O'hagan and Atsushi Yamaguchi upfront.
The battle continues on for 9 more league matches as Hibs will try to rise from ashes for its title run.

Report by Hitoshi Ono
