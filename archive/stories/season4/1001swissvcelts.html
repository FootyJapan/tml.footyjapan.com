Celts Dealt Swiss Kickin'

Misato, Sun 1st Oct. Swiss vs. Celts on a great day for a match at Misato – nice grass and a cool cloud cover meant that all the excuses in the world couldn’t help – ability was going to be the deciding factor on the day.  The Celts are a decent side, relegating the Irish players to defense and using all nationalities in attack – Czechs, Japanese, and some Latin flair.  The only Irishman in the Swiss half of the pitch was Kirk, holding down the fort for the Swiss.  The Swiss were somewhat depleted on the day with two of their best players away for the weekend; fortunately, Dierk, Dale, and Angelo made their first appearances of the season for us and were solid. 
 
The game started with the Celts looking more lively.  They held up the ball well in midfield and were connecting well with ‘So’ up front and Roberto coming through midfield.  One of the recruits from the Shogun Knights, the largest physical specimen in the TML, was dominant in the air and and useful on the ground.  He created their first chance with a nice through ball to ‘So’ who had gotten behind Dierk – Dierk couldn’t control himself and tapped him on the shoulder.  The resulting death roll was impressive and the ref. pointed to the spot.  The spot kick was placed to the right, the Swiss Doctor parried it well with a dive but ‘tit man’ Dale was admiring his fans in the stands (or maybe himself) and failed to clear the rebound, allowing his attacker to get to it first to knock it in past a stranded Peter.  Peter gave his usual howl when a mistake has been made but the score line read 1-0 to the Celts and it was deserved.
 
This seemed to spark the Swiss and some nice football resulted.  Andy came on at left back which allowed Kai to move up front while Dale in midfield evened things up a little with the Terminator lurking.  Kai went straight to work and one of his first plays was a great through pass to Karl who tried to volley it left footed only for it to just miss the right post.  The next chance fell to Kai himself and he took on two defenders, the first with pure speed and the second with a nice pull back off the end line allowing himself time to slot it in at the near post, a great individual effort.  He produced another beauty about 5 minutes later beating the same guy twice – unfortunately for Kai, the keeper stopped his frist effort but Philip followed it up and knocked it in for a 2-1 Swiss lead.  It was a great comeback after a very shakey start. The first half finished that way and the Swiss were starting to get more comfortable with the players on the pitch.
 
The second half got a little more physical with some solid tackles by Dierk and with Andy taking some offense to an elbow to the midsection.  The Celts tried to push forward more but crosses were not being met and the marking and covering by the Swiss back four were up to the task.  Going the other way, Uchida had a one on one vs. the keeper which he could have done better with while JP’s slow motion header merely floated over the bar.  It was eventually an individual effort by Angelo which clinched the 3 points for the Swiss as JP played it in to his feet, he was stopped once but kept on working and eventually found space to get off a weak left footed shot.  It looked like the keeper’s dive would be enough but it just snuck in the lower right corner.  It was a nice piece of work by Angelo.  The final 10 minutes were typical TML with the Celts pushing more men forward in the hope of sneaking a goal – fortunately the Swiss back four stayed put and the balls were picked off more and more easily.  A few counter attacks resulted but the scoreline stayed the same.  Final result 3-1, the Swiss have to keep winning with the Hibs. already establishing a large goal differential. Good effort but we have to do more as a team to score goals, relying on individual efforts won’t do it in the big games.  A final word, well done by the ref. who was consistent and on top of the game.  Both teams complained too much but he kept control and dealt with everything.

Report by Cap'n Kirk
