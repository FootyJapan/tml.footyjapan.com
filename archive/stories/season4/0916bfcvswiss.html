Swiss Goal-less Again.

OiFuto 1, Sat 16th Sept. This matchup is filled with history, unfortunately not much was added to that with a ho-hum 0-0 draw on Saturday at Oifuto.  Both teams had their defenses ready to play thus few quality chances were created and the keepers dealt pretty easily with shots from distance.  

The BFC are a stronger side than last year with some speed at the back and a young Japanese midfielder capable of bursting through the midfield – he is also capable of tumbling and doing the death roll when merely within shouting distance of an opposing player but you have to give credit to a player who takes people on (especially in the TML). 

The Swiss probably had their strongest side out so far this year but they were unable to break down a solid BFC back four.  Shots from midfield by Shio and Dave looked good but didn’t trouble the tall man between the posts (I think they call him Lurky? – I wished Sid would have been there today:-).  Kai, Yan, and Yuji offered some speedier options but the passes in the final third were cut off or were just lacking in quality due to misunderstandings.  The Swiss clearly need to play more together to create something against the stronger teams – just showing up with the same old strategy doesn’t work anymore. 

It was a solid game with some nice passing at times but neither team really threatened.  The best chance of the game fell to the BFC’s young Japanese player whose shot just went over the left hand corner with about 15 minutes to go in the 2H. 
For the Swiss – another solid game by Cho, Raffa, and Koi while Shio looked good, great stand-in keeping by Uchida as well.  Coming out of the back – the Swiss move it around nicely and are comfortable with the ball – connecting with the forwards remains an issue that lacks an obvious solution at this point.  In spite of the troubles – the Swiss move to the top of the league table for the first time in a year, good to see that order has been restored!

Report by Cap'n Kirk
