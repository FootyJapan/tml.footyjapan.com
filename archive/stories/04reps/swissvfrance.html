TML Reports

10th Jan St Mary's. Swiss Kickers 5 France FC 1.
A solid performance by the Swiss systematically took apart a tenacious France FC team, in a 5-1 win at St. Mary's last Saturday in the second game of the brand new Tokyo Metropolis League. The French without their regular keeper Elliot borrowed the Hibs keeper for the first half of the game, a decision that could've created early controversy in this embryonic league, had the result gone France's way and in the first 30 minutes, there was every chance it could.

France went on the offensive from the whistle and in the early stages of the first half were connecting some good passes and holding onto the ball effectively in midfield. They had a couple of good chances early on with shots firing just wide or over, but the Swiss - clearly a tad leg heavy after the New Year festivities - soon took control of the situation with solid defending by Kirk and Damon at the back. Aoki, playing in his usual wide role was being picked out from the back and was linking expertly with a midfield and strike force, that became noticeably more mobile as time went on.

At half time the Hibs keeper changed for a France FC outfield player and it took just seconds for Karl to break the deadlock. The second half was a different story as Yuu pushed forward setting up at least two of the Swiss goals, including a text book header by Kirk at the back post after swinging the ball in from a corner.

The French did score one consolation goal, but really looked like a different team in the second half and never looked like recovering after conceding the second of five Swiss goals. The Swiss, even with regular strikers Ian and Phil missing still looked like a formidable outfit and will definitely be THE team to beat in this new league.

Report by Jonathan Day
