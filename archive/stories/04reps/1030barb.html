NO JOY AGAIN FOR BABAS

30th Oct, YCAC. It was a wet and miserable day as Barbarians headed towards Yokohama hoping that the rain would not cancel their game against KGFC. Whilst rain did not stop play it certainly dampened down the Barbarians performance

Starting line up was Ian in goal, Fred & Fergs in central defence with Kaz & Hideki making up the back line, midfield was Richard C, Alex Durning, John Stokes & Alex Wright, with our third Alex, Red Alex up front alongside Muramoto-san, a reliable sub in Ian Pulford was on hand

KGFC were one of those sides the BaBa have struggled with all season, all Japanese, young and fast and well practised in the art of passing a ball. This was going to be no different with KGFC immediately opening up the attack and fierce defending and keeping saving BaBas on countless occasions in the first 25 mins. Inevitably a breakthrough was finally made and a breakdown in BaBas midfield gave the ball back to KGFC who finished off some decent passing with a well taken shot for a 1-0 lead. BaBas held out till half time with a few more scares but hardly a real chance of their own other than a possible penalty call as Alex W was brought down early in the game

The second half looked better for BaBas right from the off, playing with the wind on their backs BaBas had the better of the opening 10 minutes, getting their first corner of the game and certainly looking more threatening. KGFC came back into the game soon after that and were once again creating chances that the defence were just managing to contain. As the game entered the final 15 minutes BaBas pushed harder trying to find an opening leaving inevitable gaps in the midfield, one of which was exploited by KGFC with a fine run from midfield, cutting in from the right to beat Ian at the near post

BaBas again pushed forward but with two free kicks from Freddie (aka NOT Platini) being the best of our chances didn't really threaten the KGFC goal. The complete git of a keeper did get threatened from both Richard C and Red Alex but this was more verbal and physical than any real intent on his clean sheet

In the last 15 minutes Ian the keeper showed his worth with two fine short range stops that should have gone in but for his excellent keeping and he capped his performance with a great penalty save in the dying minutes as Fred was judged to have handled the ball

All in all a dissapointing day with the damp conditions leaving the BaBas attack fizzling rather than sparkling

Man of the Match : Ian the keeper
Match Reporter : Richard Carden
